# Flare Private Testing GroupMe Test Guide V0

**Operator preflight:** replace `[SUPPORT EMAIL OR CHANNEL]` and `[EXPECTED RESPONSE WINDOW]` before sending.

## Before You Start

- GroupMe is optional.
- You need an existing GroupMe account.
- You need an existing suitable GroupMe group.
- You should have permission or reasonable agreement from the people in that group.
- A test Flare sends a real message into the real selected GroupMe group.
- Notify relevant group members before you send a test.

## Safe Test Steps

1. Open the GroupMe setup area in `Customize`.
2. Confirm the selected group name carefully.
3. Review the predefined message preview.
4. Make sure you understand that the message will go into the real selected group.
5. Send a clearly marked test Flare.
6. Check GroupMe directly to verify that the message arrived in the intended group.
7. Note the time and the result you saw.

## What To Record

- the selected group name
- approximate send time
- whether the app reported success or failure
- whether the message appeared in the intended group
- whether more than one message appeared

## What Success Proves

Success only proves that Flare believes GroupMe accepted the predefined test message for the selected GroupMe group.

## What Success Does Not Prove

Success does not prove:

- that every group member got a notification
- that any person saw the message
- that any person understood it
- that any person will respond
- that future sends will always work

## If No Message Arrives

- Treat that as a real failure.
- Do not assume the group is usable yet.
- Contact `[SUPPORT EMAIL OR CHANNEL]`.

## If The Wrong Group Receives It

- Disable the channel if the build allows it.
- Stop sending further tests.
- Contact `[SUPPORT EMAIL OR CHANNEL]` immediately.

## If More Than One Message Arrives

- Stop sending more tests.
- Record how many messages appeared.
- Contact `[SUPPORT EMAIL OR CHANNEL]`.

## When Behavior Appears Unsafe

Disable the channel if you can, stop further sending, and contact `[SUPPORT EMAIL OR CHANNEL]` immediately.

## Important Boundaries

- Flare does not read replies.
- Flare does not detect acknowledgement.
- Flare does not escalate.
- Flare is not an emergency service.
- If you may be in immediate danger or need urgent help, contact local emergency services or another appropriate source of immediate assistance directly. Do not wait for a Flare or GroupMe response.
