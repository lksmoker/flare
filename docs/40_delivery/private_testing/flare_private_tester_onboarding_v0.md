## Welcome

Welcome to the Flare private test. This build is invitation-only, unfinished, and meant for a small approved tester group.

## What Flare Is

Flare is a personal support tool for difficult moments. It helps you prepare a short Flare Plan, keep your own reminder content ready, and optionally send one predefined support message to one GroupMe group you choose.

## What This Private Test Is Trying To Learn

This private test is trying to learn whether the current Flare experience is understandable, honest about its limits, and usable in realistic but controlled testing.

## Before You Begin

- Use `[SUPPORTED DEVICE AND BROWSER]`.
- Use the approved email address: `[APPROVED TESTER EMAIL]`.
- Plan for about 10 to 20 minutes for your first session.
- You do not need to create a real distress event to complete onboarding.
- If you choose to test GroupMe, remember that a test Flare sends a real message into the selected real GroupMe group.

## Eligibility Confirmation

Before continuing, confirm all of the following are true:

- You are 18 or older.
- You were directly invited to this private test.
- You understand this software is unfinished.
- You understand Flare is not an emergency service.
- You will not rely on Flare as your only way to get urgent help.
- You will avoid entering confidential information about other people without their permission.

## What Works While Signed Out

While signed out, you can still:

- Read the Welcome content.
- Understand the purpose of Flare.
- Start a local Flare experience.
- Use the built-in default plan flow instead of your saved personal plan.

Signed-out use is for orientation and local fallback. It does not mean your personal configuration, history, or support-group setup is saved to your account.

## What Signing In Enables

Signing in enables the account-backed parts of the private test, including:

- Saving and reloading your personal setup
- Saving and editing your Flare Plan
- Viewing account-backed history
- Connecting GroupMe
- Using authenticated support-channel behavior

## Configuring A Short Flare Plan

Follow this first-session sequence:

1. Read the Welcome content.
2. Sign in with `[APPROVED TESTER EMAIL]`.
3. Open `Customize`.
4. Review `Behavior Pattern` and `Anchor Note` if you want to personalize them.
5. Open `Flare Plan`.
6. Add at least one short action you could realistically follow in the moment.
7. Keep the plan short. One to three meaningful actions is enough for this test.
8. Review the order of your plan actions.
9. Save the plan.

Good plan actions are short, concrete, and easy to act on. Avoid adding large amounts of sensitive detail when a simpler reminder would work.

## Optional GroupMe Setup

GroupMe is optional. You can test Flare without connecting it.

If you do want to test GroupMe:

1. Open `Customize`.
2. Open `Support Group`.
3. Connect GroupMe.
4. Choose the group you want Flare to use.
5. Review the saved message preview.
6. Save the support group.

Read the dedicated GroupMe guide in [flare_private_testing_groupme_test_guide_v0.md](/C:/dev/Flare/docs/40_delivery/private_testing/flare_private_testing_groupme_test_guide_v0.md) before sending a test Flare.

## Sending A Test Flare

1. Tell relevant group members that you are about to send a clearly marked test message.
2. Return to the GroupMe setup area.
3. Use the test-send action.
4. Wait for the app result and then check GroupMe directly.

## Confirming The Correct GroupMe Destination

Before sending any test or real Flare through GroupMe:

1. Read the selected group name carefully.
2. Make sure it is the exact group you intended.
3. Make sure you understand who is in that group.
4. If the selected group looks wrong, stop and do not send.

## Trying A Local Flare

You can safely test the local Flare flow without creating a real emergency.

1. Return to the `Flare` screen.
2. Press `Send Flare`.
3. Review the response flow.
4. If your plan is offered, begin it and move through one or two steps.
5. If you want, complete or skip the short checkpoint or reflection flow.

## What Delivery Success Means

If GroupMe delivery reports success, it means Flare believes the provider accepted the message for the selected group.

It does not prove:

- that every group member received a notification
- that anyone saw the message
- that anyone understood the message
- that anyone is available
- that anyone will respond

## What Delivery Failure Means

If GroupMe delivery reports failure or blocked delivery, treat that as a real problem. The local Flare event may still exist, but the external message did not successfully go through.

Report failed or confusing delivery behavior using the support guide in [flare_private_testing_support_guide_v0.md](/C:/dev/Flare/docs/40_delivery/private_testing/flare_private_testing_support_guide_v0.md).

## Where To Find History And Settings

- `History` shows saved Flare Events and attached reflections when you are signed in.
- `Customize` is where you review your setup, including your Flare Plan and optional GroupMe connection.

## What To Do When Something Is Confusing Or Broken

- Stop if something looks unsafe, especially if the wrong group may receive a message.
- Take note of the approximate time, what you were trying to do, and what actually happened.
- If useful, take a screenshot after removing unrelated personal information.
- Contact `[SUPPORT EMAIL OR CHANNEL]`.
- Expected response window: `[EXPECTED RESPONSE WINDOW]`.

## Emergency Boundary

Flare is not an emergency service. It does not contact emergency services, monitor you, detect emergencies, or guarantee that anyone sees or responds to a message.

If you may be in immediate danger or need urgent help, contact local emergency services or another appropriate source of immediate assistance directly. Do not wait for a Flare or GroupMe response.
