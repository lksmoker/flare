# Flare App - Anchor Note Checkpoint

## Core Insight

When someone is in craving mode, they do not necessarily lack reasons, values, or self-knowledge. They lose access to them in the moment.

Flare should restore that access.

## Named Concept

**Anchor Note**

## Product Principle

Anchor Note preserves the user's clear-minded commitments and returns them during the craving window.

## Flare Response

When the user taps **Send Flare**, the app should do more than notify others. It should activate an immediate support protocol.

Recommended order:

1. Confirm flare sent
2. Present emergency actions
3. Remind the user why they are quitting or reducing the behavior
4. Remind the user what the behavior costs them if they continue
5. Ask for a next-10-minutes commitment or check-in

## Suggested Flow

### 1. Confirm Flare Sent

Example:

> Flare sent. You do not have to act on this urge right now.

### 2. Emergency Actions First

Emergency actions should come before deeper reflection because the user may not be ready to read or reason yet.

Example actions:

- Leave the room
- Put the phone down
- Start a 2-minute timer
- Text or call someone
- Take a walk
- Shower
- Breathe
- Block or close the trigger app
- Mark "I am safe for 10 minutes"

### 3. Why I'm Quitting

This should use the user's own words whenever possible.

Examples:

- I want my attention back.
- I do not want to lose another night to this.
- I want to wake up tomorrow without regret.
- I want to be present with the people I love.

### 4. What This Behavior Costs Me

This should be sober, specific, and non-shaming.

Example:

> If I continue, I usually lose time, feel ashamed, isolate, sleep worse, and make the next craving stronger.

The goal is not punishment. The goal is restoring access to the user's clear-minded perspective during a short-term impulse state.

### 5. Next 10 Minutes Commitment

Examples:

- I'll wait 10 minutes
- I did one emergency action
- I need more help
- I'm choosing recovery right now

## Onboarding / Setup

Anchor Note should be optional, not forced.

The app should explain:

> During a craving, it can be hard to remember why you wanted to stop or what usually happens when you continue.
>
> Flare can show your own words back to you after you send a flare.
>
> The more honest, specific, and personal you make this, the more useful it may be in the moment.

## Optional Setup Fields

- What behavior are you trying to interrupt?
- Why do you want to stop or reduce this?
- What does this behavior usually cost you?
- What do you want your future self to remember during a craving?
- What actions help you interrupt the loop?

## Setup Levels

V0 should support three paths:

1. **Skip for now**
   - User still gets generic emergency actions.

2. **Use starter prompts**
   - App provides defaults or placeholders the user can edit.

3. **Make it personal**
   - User writes their own reasons, costs, reminders, and emergency actions.

## Product Framing

**Send Flare -> Activate Anchor Note**

Alternative product line:

> When craving narrows your thinking, Flare brings your anchor note back.

## Status

Checkpointed as an early product direction for the Flare App.
