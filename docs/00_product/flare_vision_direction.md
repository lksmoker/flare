# Flare Vision Direction

Status: draft
Doc Type: product vision
Role: Define the core direction, purpose, and product intent for Flare.

## Purpose

Flare is a personal interruption and recovery-support app for moments when a user is about to fall into a behavior pattern they want to stop, reduce, or interrupt.

The core idea is simple: when the user feels the pull toward the behavior, they press **Send Flare**. The app immediately helps disrupt the spiral, surfaces clear-minded recovery prompts, and gives the user a concrete next action.

Flare is not intended to shame the user, lecture them, or overcomplicate the moment. It should feel like a trusted emergency handrail.

## Problem

Many unwanted behaviors happen in a compressed window of time.

A user may know, clearly and sincerely, that they do not want to continue a pattern. But in the moment of craving, compulsion, avoidance, anxiety, or emotional overwhelm, that clear-minded knowledge can become hard to access.

Examples may include:

- compulsive scrolling
- YouTube/news feed spirals
- relapse-prone recovery behaviors
- avoidance behaviors
- anxiety-driven loops
- depressive withdrawal patterns
- other behaviors the user wants help interrupting

The app exists to help in the specific moment where insight alone is not enough.

## Core Moment

The primary product moment is:

> “I am about to do the thing I do not want to do.”

The user should be able to open Flare quickly, press a single clear action, and immediately receive support.

This moment should be treated as urgent, emotionally loaded, and low-capacity. The user should not need to configure, explain, write, think deeply, or navigate a complex UI at that moment.

## Product Loop

The core loop is:

1. **Trigger**
   - The user notices an urge, craving, spiral, avoidance pattern, or risk moment.

2. **Send Flare**
   - The user presses the primary action.

3. **Interrupt**
   - The app creates friction against the behavior and breaks the automatic loop.

4. **Recover**
   - The app gives the user a specific recovery action, prompt, or next step.

5. **Remember**
   - The app surfaces the user’s own clear-minded Anchor Note.

6. **Checkpoint**
   - The app optionally records what happened and helps the user notice patterns over time.

## Anchor Note

Anchor Note is a core part of the product.

The user should be able to set up, while clear-minded, a personal memory bank that includes:

- why they want to stop or reduce the behavior
- what continuing the behavior costs them
- what they want their future self to remember
- emergency actions that help in the moment
- people, places, phrases, or reminders that reconnect them to their values

During craving mode, Flare should return these prompts to the user.

The setup should be optional, but strongly encouraged. The product should communicate that the more honest, specific, and personal the Anchor Note is, the more useful Flare becomes in the moment.

## V0 Direction

V0 should prove that Flare is useful even without support-group or Telegram integration.

V0 should include:

- account/login
- behavior or pattern configuration
- a simple Send Flare action
- predefined disruptive actions
- Anchor Note setup
- recovery prompts surfaced during a flare
- lightweight flare history
- basic reflection/checkpointing

The first version should focus on the solo user experience.

## V1 Direction

V1 should introduce outside support.

The likely first integration is Telegram because it may offer a relatively low-friction way for users to connect a support group, trusted person, or recovery contact.

V1 should explore:

- connecting Telegram
- setting up a support group or contact
- configuring what message gets sent
- choosing when a message is sent
- support escalation rules
- preserving user control and consent

The support flow should remain optional. Flare should still work for users who do not yet have people in their support network.

## Long-Term Possibilities

Longer-term directions may include:

- support for recovery groups of many kinds
- optional community support
- anonymous or invite-based groups
- depression/anxiety variants
- quarterly or annual progress checkpoints
- onboarding surveys
- outcome tracking
- pattern insights
- templates for specific behavior categories
- integration with human coaches, sponsors, therapists, or peer groups where appropriate

## Product Principles

- The user is not bad for struggling.
- The app should reduce shame, not increase it.
- The urgent moment should require almost no thinking.
- The user’s own clear-minded words should matter more than generic advice.
- Support should be configurable and consent-based.
- V0 should be useful without social features.
- The product should be small, direct, and emotionally safe.
